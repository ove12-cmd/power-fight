Mixed Martial Arts
Mixed Martial Arts combines striking (punches, kicks, elbows, knees), wrestling (takedowns, clinch), and ground fighting (submissions, escapes) into one complete system. Classes at Power Fight cover all ranges — stand-up, clinch, and ground — so you develop a well-rounded game.
No. Beginners are welcome. Prior boxing, BJJ, or wrestling experience helps but is absolutely not required. Coaches build fundamentals from zero and scale training to your level.
Training is controlled and structured — it is not a street fight. Sparring is optional, always supervised, and only introduced when you're ready. The vast majority of class time is technique, drilling, and pad work.
For your first sessions: comfortable athletic wear and water. As you continue you'll want boxing gloves (14–16 oz), hand wraps, a mouthguard, and shin guards. We have loaner gear available while you get started.
Grappling
Takedowns, control positions, submissions, and escapes — all no-gi. The curriculum draws from wrestling, BJJ, and Judo and applies them to MMA context: cage awareness, ground-and-pound defence, and stand-up transitions are all part of it.
No. All classes are no-gi. Shorts and a rash guard or fitted t-shirt are all you need.
Similar foundation, different focus. Grappling here prioritises MMA application — cage control, takedown defence, and submission awareness in a fight context — rather than a points-based sport ruleset.
It's demanding but scalable. Coaches match beginners with suitable partners and intensity builds gradually. Your cardio and body awareness improve quickly once you start.
Striking
Punches, kicks, elbows, and knees drawn from boxing, Muay Thai, and kickboxing — applied to MMA rules. Classes cover distance management, combinations, footwork, and how to create takedown opportunities from the stand-up.
No. Technique is built from scratch. Beginners and experienced strikers train together — coaches scale the complexity per person.
Boxing gloves (14–16 oz), hand wraps, and a mouthguard. Shin guards once you start pad work. We have loaners for your first sessions.
Most students develop solid fundamentals within 2–3 months of consistent training. Striking rewards repetition — the more you drill, the faster it clicks.
Wrestling
Single and double-leg takedowns, level changes, clinch control, sprawls, and scrambles. Wrestling is the backbone of MMA positioning — it determines who decides where the fight happens.
Not at all. Most students come in with zero wrestling background. The skills taught here focus on practical MMA application, so no prior sport wrestling experience is needed.
Wrestling focuses on the stand-up and the transition to the ground — takedowns, clinch control, and cage work. Grappling takes over once you're on the mat, covering submissions and ground control. Both complement each other perfectly.
Shorts and a fitted rash guard or t-shirt. Wrestling shoes are optional — most train barefoot. A mouthguard is recommended once you start live drilling.
Les Mills Bodycombat
Bodycombat is a high-energy group fitness class inspired by martial arts — boxing, karate, Muay Thai, and more — set to motivating music. It's non-contact, follows a choreographed programme, and delivers a full-body cardio workout.
None at all. The moves are taught step by step as part of the class. It's designed for everyone, regardless of fitness level or fighting background.
It's a proper cardio workout — expect to sweat. The intensity is self-regulated, so you push as hard as you choose. Most people leave feeling energised rather than broken.
Comfortable training shoes (cross-trainers or gym shoes work well), athletic clothes, and water. No gloves or pads required — this is non-contact.
Strength & Conditioning
Gym training designed specifically for combat sports. Every exercise targets the explosiveness, endurance, and injury resistance that fighting demands. Not a standard fitness class — everything has a purpose for athletic performance.
No. Load and intensity are individually scaled. Beginners are coached on movement quality before any weight is added.
Yes — that's the intent. S&C is designed to make you better at everything else. Athletes who combine it with striking or grappling progress significantly faster than those who only train one or the other.
Comfortable training clothes and athletic shoes. No special equipment required — everything you need is in the gym.
Performance Training
Advanced training sessions built for athletes who compete or want to push to the next level. Structured sparring, fight simulation, pad rounds, and high-intensity drills — all coach-led and designed to peak performance.
Athletes preparing for competition or those who have a strong foundation and want to train at a higher intensity. It's not for absolute beginners — a solid technical base across striking and/or grappling is expected.
Higher intensity, more live rounds, and a focus on putting everything together under pressure. Regular MMA classes build the tools; Performance Training sharpens them for real use.
Talk to your coach. If you've been training consistently for 6+ months and feel solid in your fundamentals, it's worth discussing. The coaches will tell you honestly when you're ready to step up.
Personal Training
One-on-one sessions tailored entirely to your goals — whether that's learning a specific discipline, accelerating progress in an area, preparing for competition, weight loss, or general fitness. The coach focuses 100% on you.
It depends on your goals and schedule. One session per week combined with group classes is a great starting point for most people. Your coach will help you plan the right frequency.
Absolutely — and this is the most effective approach. Group classes build your skills alongside training partners; PT sessions let you drill weak points and get individual feedback at a much deeper level.
Contact us via the contact page or speak directly to a coach after any group class. We'll match you with the right coach and find a time that works for you.
Kettlebell Training
Structured training with kettlebells covering swings, cleans, presses, snatches, and carries. It builds functional strength, explosive power, grip endurance, and full-body conditioning — all highly transferable to martial arts.
No. The class starts with technique fundamentals. The hip hinge and swing are taught in detail before any load is added, so you build correct movement patterns from day one.
Kettlebell training combines strength and conditioning in the same movement. You're working power, endurance, and stability simultaneously — it's more athletic and dynamic than machine-based gym work, and directly complements fighting training.
Your coach will recommend the right starting weight after seeing your movement. Most beginners start lighter than they expect — technique matters far more than load at the start.
Kids Training
From 5 years old. Kids classes are specifically designed for that age group with age-appropriate drills, games, and structure.

Warm-up games, technique drills, partner pad work, and cool-down stretching. Sessions are designed to keep kids engaged and moving, not standing in a line waiting their turn.
Focus, respect, listening, perseverance, and confidence. The life skills that come from structured martial arts training tend to show up at school and at home — parents notice the difference quickly.
